How You Can Help

Indoor Conservation

  • Fix leaky faucets immediately
  • Install low-flow showerheads
  • Run full loads of laundry and dishes
  • Turn off water while brushing teeth

Outdoor Conservation

  • Water lawns early morning or evening
  • Use native plants that require less water
  • Install drip irrigation systems
  • Use mulch to retain soil moisture

Smart Practices

  • Collect rainwater for gardens
  • Use brooms instead of hoses to clean
  • Check for pool leaks regularly
  • Install smart irrigation controllers

Learn About Florida's Water Ecosystems

Understanding our water resources is the first step to protecting them.

Understanding Red Tide

Red tide is caused by Karenia brevis, a microscopic alga that produces powerful neurotoxins. Learn how it affects marine life and human health.

Can cause respiratory irritation in humans
Kills fish and marine mammals
Most common from September to February

Florida's Aquifer System

The Floridan Aquifer is one of the most productive aquifers in the world, providing drinking water to millions of Floridians.

Covers 100,000 square miles
Provides 90% of Florida's drinking water
Threatened by over-pumping and pollution

Marine Ecosystems

Florida's coastal waters support diverse marine ecosystems including coral reefs, seagrass beds, and mangrove forests.

Florida Reef is the only barrier reef in US
Seagrass beds support fish nurseries
Mangroves protect coastlines from erosion

Water Cycle & Climate

Florida's water cycle is influenced by tropical weather patterns, sea level rise, and changing precipitation patterns.

Average rainfall: 54 inches per year
Sea level rising 1 inch every 3 years
More intense droughts and floods expected
